**Section: "Why active recall works"**

Write this section covering:

- **The testing effect**: Reference Roediger and Karpicke (2006) - retrieving information from memory strengthens long-term retention more than re-reading the same material. Their experiments showed students who practiced retrieval retained significantly more after one week compared to students who re-studied.

- **Learning techniques review**: Reference Dunlosky et al. (2013) - a comprehensive review of ten learning techniques. Practice testing and distributed practice were rated as having the highest utility. Highlighting, re-reading, and summarization were rated low.

- **Retrieval vs concept mapping**: Reference Karpicke and Blunt (2011) - retrieval practice produced better results than elaborative concept mapping for meaningful learning.

- **How the plugin connects to this research** - tie each feature to the science:
  - Concept map: gives you a structural overview of what you will be tested on, orienting your thinking before retrieval begins
  - Questions ordered simple to complex: builds retrieval from foundational concepts up, following the scaffolding principle
  - Hints that cue without revealing: support retrieval without short-circuiting the effort that makes it effective
  - Reference answers with sources: let you verify your recall and trace back to original notes for deeper review

Use plain prose, not academic jargon. This is for Obsidian users, not researchers. Keep it concise - aim for ~150-200 words total for this section. Include the three paper references as inline citations (Author, Year) within the prose, not as a separate bibliography.

**Section: "How is this different from LLM Test Generator?"**

Write a short section (~80-120 words) explaining the difference from "LLM Test Generator" (Competence) by Aldo George:

- That plugin requires you to type full answers into text boxes, then submits them to an AI for grading and scoring
- This plugin generates questions designed to be answered out loud or in your head - zero typing, zero grading, pure retrieval practice
- Key differences to highlight:
  - No test anxiety from scores - the goal is recall, not performance
  - Effort goes into retrieval itself, not writing and waiting for grading
  - Concept map for orientation before testing
  - Questions ordered from simple to complex
  - Hints that cue without revealing the answer

Write in a factual, non-competitive tone. Acknowledge the other plugin exists and serves a different approach.
